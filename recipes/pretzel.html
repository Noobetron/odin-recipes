Pretzel

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons baking soda
  • 1 cup self-rising flour, plus more for kneading
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 3 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon coarse salt, or as needed

Directions

Step 1

Bring 6 cups of water to a low boil in a large pot. Add baking soda.

Step 2

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease a baking sheet.

Step 3

Mix 1 cup flour and Greek yogurt together in a medium bowl until a shaggy dough forms.
Transfer to a surface dusted with more self-rising flour, and knead for 8 to 10 minutes.
Continue adding flour as needed if the dough is too sticky.

Step 4

Cut the dough into 4 equal pieces. Roll each segment into a long, thin rope, 22 to 24 inches in length.
Twist the rope into a pretzel shape by taking the two ends, twisting them around each other, then securing
both ends side-by-side onto the center portion of the rope.

Step 5

One by one, carefully drop each pretzel into the water and boil until the pretzel floats to the top, 2 to 3 minutes.
Drain on paper towels or a cooling rack.

Step 6

Transfer to the prepared baking sheet.

Step 7

Bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes. Remove from the oven and increase the oven's temperature to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).
Brush the pretzels with some of the melted butter; continue to bake until golden brown, 4 to 5 minutes more. Brush with remaining butter
and top with coarse salt.
